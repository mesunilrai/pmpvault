Domain Overview

Domain 1 focuses on leading the project team. PMI expects you to act as a servant leader: removing obstacles, building psychological safety, resolving conflict, and enabling high performance across diverse and virtual teams.

T1
Manage Conflict
Interpret the source and stage of conflict, apply techniques to resolve it.
▾
Conflict Resolution Team Dynamics Emotional Intelligence
Key Concepts
Thomas-Kilmann Model Collaborating / Problem-Solving Compromising Avoiding / Forcing Smoothing / Accommodating Conflict Stages Active Listening

Predictive

PM mediates formally; documented resolution in team charter.

Agile

Team self-resolves; Scrum Master removes impediments and facilitates retrospectives.

Hybrid

Team norms address self-resolution; PM escalates only when team cannot resolve.

🎯 Exam Tip

PMI's preferred conflict resolution is Collaborating / Problem-Solving — address the root cause, create win-win outcomes. Forcing is a last resort. If asked "what should you do first," choose collaboration before escalation.

T2

▾
Servant Leadership Situational Leadership Vision & Mission
Key Concepts
Servant Leadership Emotional Intelligence (EI) Situational Leadership Directing / Coaching / Supporting / Delegating Psychological Safety Diversity & Inclusion Growth Mindset

Predictive

PM directs work per plan; authority flows from PMO hierarchy.

Agile

Servant leader coaches self-organizing team; Product Owner owns vision.

🎯 Exam Tip

PMI favors servant leadership heavily. Always think: "How do I empower the team?" not "How do I direct them?" Situational leadership — adjust style to the team member's competence and commitment level.

▾

Predictive

When a team member is underperforming, PMI expects you to coach first — not discipline immediately. Understand the root cause before escalating to HR or removing them from the project.

▾

Key Concepts
Delegation Matrix Accountability vs Responsibility Trust Building Decision Authority Strengths-Based Assignments RACI Chart

Predictive

PM delegates clearly defined tasks; authority documented in RACI.

Agile

PMI wants you to trust your team. Don't micromanage. Empower people with the information and authority they need. In agile contexts, teams are self-organizing — the PM/SM creates the environment, not the assignments.

▾

Key Concepts

Predictive

Issue log maintained; PM escalates blockers per governance structure.

Agile

▾
Negotiation Contracts Stakeholder Management
Key Concepts
BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement) Principled Negotiation Win-Win Outcomes Contract Types (FFP, T&M, CPFF) Scope Negotiation Resource Negotiation

Predictive

Formal procurement; legal review of contracts; change control for modifications.

Agile

Agile contracts favor T&M or outcome-based; team negotiates sprint commitments.

Hybrid

🎯 Exam Tip

PMI prefers win-win (integrative) negotiation over zero-sum (distributive). Understand the other party's interests, not just positions. Know your BATNA — it's your walk-away power.

T13

▾
Mentoring Knowledge Transfer Leadership Development
Key Concepts
Coaching vs Mentoring (difference) Knowledge Transfer Succession Planning Leadership Pipeline Informal Mentoring Reverse Mentoring

Predictive

PM formally mentors junior PMs; knowledge transfer planned at project close.

Agile

Senior developers pair-program with juniors; Scrum Master coaches team on agile practices.

Hybrid

🎯 Exam Tip

Mentoring ≠ Coaching: Mentoring = sharing your experience and expertise; Coaching = asking questions to help someone discover their own solutions. PMI expects PMs to do both. Don't confuse them on exam questions.

T14

Assess team member's emotional state; use EI to adapt communication and foster collaboration.
▾
Emotional Intelligence Empathy Self-Awareness
Key Concepts
Goleman's 5 EI Components Self-Awareness Self-Regulation Motivation Empathy Social Skills Psychological Safety Reading the Room

Predictive

PM uses EI during status meetings, performance reviews, and conflict situations.

Agile

Retrospective mood boards, health checks, and team sentiment tracking. SM gauges team energy.

Hybrid

🎯 Exam Tip

EI is tested throughout the exam — not just in "soft skills" questions. When a team member seems disengaged or a stakeholder is hostile, apply EI first: acknowledge feelings, understand the root cause, then act. Don't jump straight to solutions.

PMI Mindset — What the Exam Expects
  • Servant Leadership always wins. PMI wants you removing obstacles and empowering your team, not commanding and controlling.
  • Resolve conflict collaboratively. Problem-solving (win-win) is the preferred approach. Forcing is almost always wrong on the exam.
  • Self-organizing teams. In agile questions, teams decide their own work. Don't assign tasks for them — create the environment.
  • Coach before disciplining. If someone is underperforming, the first step is always a coaching conversation, not HR escalation.
  • Engage stakeholders continuously. Stakeholder management isn't a one-time activity — it's ongoing throughout the project.
  • EI over authority. When in doubt between an emotional/relational response and an authority/formal response, choose EI.
  • Virtual teams need intentional connection. Don't assume remote teams will build trust naturally — the PM must create it deliberately.
  • Ground rules are co-created. Never impose team norms — build them collaboratively so the team owns them.
  • Hybrid = context matters. For hybrid questions, think: What approach fits this specific situation? Predictive for stable, agile for uncertain.
  • Domain 1 = 42% of exam. Most situational "what would you do?" questions live here. If a question involves people, teams, or leadership — think servant leadership first.
